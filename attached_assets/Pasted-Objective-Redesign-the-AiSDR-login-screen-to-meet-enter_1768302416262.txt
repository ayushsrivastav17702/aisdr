Objective:
Redesign the AiSDR login screen to meet enterprise software standards, prioritizing trust, control, and seriousness over marketing or visual excitement.

This is NOT a marketing page.
This is NOT a landing page.
This is NOT a branding experiment.

1️⃣ CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

The login screen must communicate:

Stability

Security

Control

Enterprise readiness

It must not:

Sell features

Explain the product

Use marketing language

Use large gradients, illustrations, or split-screen layouts

If a design choice increases “wow” but reduces “trust” → reject it.

2️⃣ LAYOUT REQUIREMENTS
✅ Required

Single-column, centered layout

Login card is the primary and dominant element

Calm, neutral background (light or very subtle gradient)

Login action visually dominant

❌ Forbidden

Split-screen layouts

Feature bullet points

Hero copy

Product marketing sections

Large background gradients

“AI-Powered”, “Modern”, “Revolutionary” language

3️⃣ CONTENT RULES (VERY IMPORTANT)
Allowed Text (MAXIMUM)

You may include only:

Product name/logo

“Welcome back”

ONE short positioning line (max 12 words)

Login buttons

Subtle trust indicators

Approved Positioning Line Examples (Pick ONE)

“Secure outbound execution platform for revenue teams”

“Enterprise outbound platform with role-based access”

“Sales execution system for SDRs and revenue teams”

❌ Do NOT add:

Feature lists

AI explanations

Benefits

Use cases

4️⃣ TRUST SIGNALS (MANDATORY, SUBTLE)

Under the login buttons, add small, quiet text such as:

SOC2-aligned • Multi-tenant • Role-based access • SSO ready

Rules:

No icons

No badges

No hover effects

No links

This is reassurance, not promotion

5️⃣ AUTH OPTIONS

Google Sign-In is allowed

Email login is allowed

Do NOT make Google visually dominant over email

Do NOT mention “Contact support” unless unavoidable

If access is invite-only, phrase it calmly:

“Access is managed by your organization administrator”

6️⃣ VISUAL TONE
Must Feel Like:

Salesforce

Atlassian

Linear

Notion (authenticated areas)

Must NOT Feel Like:

Webflow landing page

Startup marketing site

Pitch deck

AI demo app

7️⃣ WHAT NOT TO TOUCH

Authentication logic

Role routing

Session handling

Security flows

Existing APIs

This is UI and copy only.

8️⃣ SUCCESS CRITERIA (TEST THIS)

Show the login screen to:

A CTO

A VP Sales

An Enterprise IT admin

If their reaction is:

“Okay, this looks solid.”

You succeeded.

If their reaction is:

“What does this tool do?”
or
“Why am I seeing marketing copy?”

You failed.

🚫 ABSOLUTE FAIL CONDITIONS

The task is considered FAILED if:

Features are listed on the login screen

Large gradients dominate the page

Marketing copy exceeds one sentence

Login action feels secondary to visuals

✅ FINAL GOAL (ONE SENTENCE)

Make the login screen feel like entering a control room, not a sales pitch.
